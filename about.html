Biography

Security Researcher

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Khoury College of Computer Sciences at the Northeastern University. At Northeastern, I am advised by Alina Oprea. I obtained my doctorate degree in Computer Science from the Department of Computer Science at University of Central Florida (UCF). At UCF, I was advised by David Mohaisen.

I am interested in the wide area of security and privacy. Particularly, I enjoy building tools that help ease efforts at the different stages of incident response life cycle. My recent efforts have involved malware analysis, vulnerability management, machine learning applications, and analysis of the evolving threat landscape.

My current research explores the global threat landscape measurement, investiagting the persistent attack vectors and prevalent attack strategies. I have developed an Open Source Cyber Threat Intelligence (OSCTI) framework to infer attack and campaign attributes for the network-based alerts.

My PhD research investiagted robust detection of IoT malware, affinities among IoT threat actors and intermediary sources of of attack, and reliable dissemination of vulnerabilities. Our efforts resulted in IoT malware attack lifecycle reconstruction, ML-based IoT malware detectors, adversarial attacks on malware detectors, and lightweight malware campaign identifier through their execution traces. Additionally, we also assessed the reliability of public the National Vulnerability Database (NVD). We identified and fixed the incompleteness and inconsistencies in them. Our results where shared with the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST).

Before starting my grad school, I worked as a Data Analyst at Apple. Earlier, I obtained my undergrad degree in Electronics and Communication Engineering from the Jamia Millia Islamia (JMI) university in New Delhi, India.
