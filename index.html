I am looking for research positions in the industry or academia. Feel free to get in touch with your suggestions and/or opportunities!

Hello! I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Khoury College of Computer Sciences at the Northeastern University. At Northeastern, I am advised by Prof. Alina Oprea. I recently completed my doctorate degree in Computer Science from the Department of Computer Science at University of Central Florida (UCF). At UCF, I was advised by Prof. David Mohaisen. I am working on augmenting learning algorithms to defend against the evolving malware threats.

I am interested in the wide area of security and privacy. My research focus in the recent past has been on malware analysis, vulnerability management, and analysis of the evolving threat landscape. Currently, I am working on global threat landscape measurement by leveraging the alerts raised by a globally distributed network of honeypots deployed by a industry giant in cyber-security.

My PhD research focused on malware analysis and vulnerability management. I worked on designing effective categorization and detection of the malicious software targeting the Internet of Things (IoT devices). We also explored practical attacks to such Machine Learning (ML)-based detection systems. Additionally, we also investigated the applicability of public vulnerability databases (National Vulnerability Database) to emphasize upon their incompleteness and inconsistencies in them. Our results where shared with the National Institute of Science and Technology (NIST).

In earlier life, I have worked as a Data Analyst (C) at Apple. Before, I obtained my Bachelors of Technology degree in Electronics and Communication Engineering from the Jamia Millia Islamia (JMI), in New Delhi, India.
